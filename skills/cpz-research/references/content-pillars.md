# CPZ Fitness — Content Pillars Reference

All CPZ Fitness social content maps to one of these four pillars. Every pillar has a defined ICA trigger, hook formula patterns, and a bank of example topics.

---

## Pillar 1: Data-Driven Insights

**What it does:** Reframes how the ICA thinks about fitness using numbers, metrics, and research. Posts trigger recognition — "I didn't know that" or "That's literally me."

**Lead with:** The stat or myth first, then give the plain-spoken take on why it matters.

**Hook formula patterns:**
- `[Surprising number] about [familiar topic].` → "You lose 3–8% of muscle mass per decade after 30. It starts earlier than you think."
- `[Common belief] is [wrong/backwards].` → "BMI is not a fitness metric. It's a population census tool."
- `[X years] of [effort] and I [missed obvious thing].` → "I tracked macros for 2 years and never once tracked my protein. No wonder nothing changed."
- `If you're [relatable situation], it's a [pattern] problem — not a willpower problem.`

**Example topics (10+):**
1. BMI limitations for skinny-fat body types — what BMI actually measures
2. The 2–6 lb/year slow fat gain pattern and why it's invisible until it isn't
3. Body recomposition metrics — how to measure progress when the scale lies
4. Protein targets by bodyweight — the actual math, not the bro-science
5. Sleep and muscle recovery data — why 6 hours is costing you gains
6. The minimum effective dose of strength training for busy professionals
7. Metabolic rate myths — why "starvation mode" is misunderstood
8. How long it actually takes to lose muscle from detraining (more than you think)
9. Desk posture and its measurable impact on strength output
10. Why NEAT (non-exercise activity thermogenesis) matters more than gym sessions for desk workers
11. The gap between caloric needs for sedentary vs. active people — most people overestimate
12. Cortisol's real role in body composition for high-stress professionals

**Platform fit:** Strongest on Instagram (carousels), LinkedIn (data framing as professional insight), Threads/BlueSky (one-liner stat hooks)

**Seasonal angle triggers:**
- New Year: "Resolution fitness math doesn't add up. Here's why." (data on January gym abandonment)
- Summer: Body recomposition timelines — setting realistic expectations
- Q4 sprint season: Stress and cortisol data during high-workload periods

## Pillar 2: Workout Tips & Micro-Workouts

**What it does:** Delivers practical, immediately actionable training content optimized for the time-limited ICA. Home-friendly over gym-dependent. RPE-based framing over rigid rep schemes.

**Lead with:** The time or equipment constraint first — "15 minutes. No equipment." — then deliver the system.

**Hook formula patterns:**
- `[X minutes]. [Zero/minimal equipment]. Here's the stack.`
- `The workout I do when I have [specific constraint] and still need to hit [goal].`
- `Stop [overcomplicated approach]. Do [simple thing] instead.`
- `Your desk is destroying [specific physical outcome]. Here's the fix.`

**Example topics (10+):**
1. 15-minute home pull workout — no pull-up bar required
2. Desk mobility sequence for people who sit 10+ hours/day
3. How to make progress training only 3 days per week
4. RPE explained for people who've never heard of it
5. The push/pull/legs split for the minimalist — stripped to essentials
6. Micro-workouts during the workday — 5-minute desk resets
7. Progressive overload when you don't have a full gym — bodyweight version
8. How to warm up in under 5 minutes and not skip it
9. The Big 5 lifts and why everything else is optional
10. Training during your busiest weeks — minimum viable protocol
11. Deload week explained — why rest IS the work
12. Form cues for the deadlift for people who've only seen it done wrong

**Platform fit:** Strongest on Instagram (carousels and Reels), Facebook (workout sequences), TikTok (Reel demonstrations)

**Seasonal angle triggers:**
- January: "Gym is packed. Here's what to do at home instead."
- Summer travel: "Training in a hotel room — the minimum viable protocol"
- Holiday period: "Maintaining during December without losing your mind"

## Pillar 3: Nutrition & Protein Strategy

**What it does:** Provides macro education and protein-first framing. No rigid meal plans — a flexible, repeatable approach to food. Hits close to the ICA's actual problem: he knows nutrition matters but feels overwhelmed by conflicting information.

**Lead with:** A number that triggers recognition, or a myth the ICA believes that's actively holding them back.

**Hook formula patterns:**
- `[Protein target] grams. That's what you need per day. Here's how to actually hit it.`
- `[Nutrition myth] isn't just wrong — it's why you're spinning your wheels.`
- `You're not failing at nutrition. You're following a broken approach.`
- `I used to [overcomplicated approach]. Then I fixed one variable and everything changed.`

**Example topics (10+):**
1. How to pack 50g of protein into breakfast — real meal options with macros shown
2. The actual math on protein per meal — how much can you absorb at once?
3. Why meal timing is a distraction from the real problem (total daily protein)
4. Protein sources ranked by effort-to-payoff ratio — what busy guys actually eat
5. How to hit protein targets without meal prepping every Sunday
6. The "I don't have time to cook" nutrition system — minimum viable healthy eating
7. Alcohol and body composition — the honest math, not the moralist's lecture
8. Eating during your busiest weeks — maintaining nutrition when life gets hectic
9. Why you don't need a rigid meal plan — a flexible, repeatable approach to food
10. Caloric surplus vs. deficit — how to know which phase you're in
11. The one supplement that's actually worth taking (protein powder) and one that isn't (most things)
12. Intuitive eating for over-thinkers — how to track without tracking

**Platform fit:** Strongest on Instagram (carousels with macro math), LinkedIn (professional confidence angle), Facebook (conversational format with practical tips)

**Seasonal angle triggers:**
- New Year: "Before you start another diet, fix this one thing first."
- Summer: Lean gain vs. cut — decision framework
- Holiday: "How to handle the holiday meals without destroying progress or your relationships"

## Pillar 4: Consistency & Habit Systems

**What it does:** Addresses the psychology and mechanics of showing up. Reframes gym avoidance as a habit problem, not a motivation problem. The ICA already knows what to do — this pillar fixes why he's not doing it.

**Lead with:** The relatable failure or the reframe. The ICA needs to recognize himself in the opening, not feel lectured at.

**Hook formula patterns:**
- `You're not skipping the gym because you're lazy. You're skipping it because [specific pattern that's breaking down].`
- `I skipped [X sessions/weeks] in a row. Here's what I actually did about it.`
- `[Common productivity insight] works everywhere else in your life. It also works for training. Here's how.`
- `The reason most people quit in [month] has nothing to do with motivation.`

**Example topics (10+):**
1. Why you keep skipping the gym — the real reason (not laziness)
2. The two-minute rule for training — how to start on your worst days
3. How to build a training habit when your schedule is controlled chaos
4. What to do when you miss a week — the re-entry protocol
5. Habit stacking — binding workouts to existing triggers
6. The smallest version that still works — why tiny consistent effort beats big sporadic pushes
7. Why motivation is the wrong metric — consistency as a practice, not a mood
8. Removing friction before the moment of decision — setting yourself up in advance
9. Why scheduling the workout IS the behavior — not just a reminder
10. Identity shift: from "I'm trying to work out" to "I'm someone who trains"
11. How to handle the "I'll restart Monday" loop — and break it permanently
12. Accountability systems for people who hate accountability — the solo operator's version

**Platform fit:** Strongest on Instagram (carousels and Reels with hooks), LinkedIn (productivity framing), Threads/BlueSky (punchy reframes), Facebook (conversational engagement posts)

**Seasonal angle triggers:**
- January: The restart loop — why this year will be different (and what makes it actually different)
- Mid-year: "You started strong in January. Here's what happened and what to do now."
- Q4: Maintaining through high-workload season — the minimum effective protocol

## Research Workflow

When brainstorming content angles from a raw idea:

1. **State the raw input** — a topic, a data point, a thing you observed
2. **Map it to a pillar** — which of the 4 pillars does it belong to?
3. **Generate 3–5 angle variants** using the hook formulas for that pillar
4. **Platform-match each angle** — which platform does each angle perform best on?
5. **Select the lead angle** — which triggers the most recognition for the ICA?

Output format for a research session:
```
Pillar: [Name]
Platform: [Target platform]
Raw topic: [One sentence]
Angles:
  1. [Hook formula applied]
  2. [Hook formula applied]
  3. [Hook formula applied]
Best fit: [Angle + platform recommendation]
```
