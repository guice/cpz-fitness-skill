## The 4-Phase Roadmap

A strategic 4-phase periodization model, ensuring progressive overload while prioritizing joint health and recovery — crucial for men over 35.

### Phase 1 — Foundation & Form (Weeks 1–4)

**Focus:** Mastery & Joint Health

We establish a robust baseline using bodyweight and resistance bands. The goal is to master movement patterns, improve mobility, and prepare the body for heavier loads without risking injury.

### Phase 2 — Progressive Hypertrophy (Weeks 5–8)

**Focus:** Muscle Building

We introduce moderate weight training (6–12 rep ranges). The focus shifts to controlled, consistent progressive overload (increasing load by 2.5–5% weekly) to stimulate visible muscle growth.

### Phase 3 — Strength & Density (Weeks 9–12)

**Focus:** Power & Recomposition

We lower the rep ranges slightly (4–8) to build foundational strength. This phase accelerates body recomposition, utilizing a slight caloric deficit or maintenance to burn fat while preserving the new muscle.

### Phase 4 — Peak & Polish (Weeks 13–16)

**Focus:** Intensification

We implement advanced techniques (supersets, drop sets) to maximize hypertrophy and break through any plateaus. This phase solidifies the visible transformation and prepares you for long-term maintenance.

## Nutrition Strategy: Flexible Fuel

We abandon the restrictive "good vs. bad food" mentality. Our approach is built on **flexible dieting (IIFYM)** and **calorie/carbohydrate cycling**, designed to fit your life, not consume it.

- **The Recomposition Framework:** A slight caloric deficit on rest days and maintenance/slight surplus on intense training days. This allows us to lose fat and build muscle simultaneously.
- **Protein as the Anchor:** Target 1.6–2.4g of protein per kg of body weight (e.g., 125–175g for a 175lb man), distributed across 3–5 meals to maximize muscle protein synthesis.
- **Navigating the Social Scene:** "Banking" strategies for social events — how to enjoy alcohol (opting for lower-calorie choices) and dining out without derailing progress.
