# CPZ Fitness — Reel Formats Reference

## Core Philosophy

Reels and TikTok scripts are not carousels with a voice-over. They are a distinct format built on spoken delivery, pacing, and visual performance. The Sarcastic Sage voice works in video — but it requires translation. Dry delivery, deliberate pauses, and specific visual cues replace the design hierarchy of a graphic.

**The 2-second hook rule:** The first thing Philip says on camera IS the hook. No "hey what's up guys," no "in this video," no lead-up. The first spoken words must be the complete hook — the statement that makes someone stop scrolling. If the first 2 seconds don't earn the watch, the algorithm buries it.

---

## Script Notation

Use this format for all reel scripts:

```
[VISUAL: description of what's on screen]
PHILIP: "Spoken dialogue exactly as delivered."
[PAUSE: 1 beat] or [CUT TO: next visual]
[TEXT ON SCREEN: overlay text if applicable]
```

**On-camera vs. voice-over:**
- **On-camera** — Philip speaking directly to lens. Use for personal stories, myth-busts, and habit/psychology posts where his credibility and presence are the content.
- **Voice-over** — Philip narrating while visuals or text carry the story. Use for workout demos, data breakdowns, or "here's what worked" posts where showing beats telling.

## The 4 Reel Lengths

### 15-Second — One-Liner Punch

**Structure:**
- Hook (2s) → Punchline or reframe (8s) → Single text CTA on screen (5s)

**Beat template:**
```
[ON-CAMERA]
PHILIP: "[Hook statement — complete thought, unexpected angle]"
[PAUSE: deliberate, 1–2 beats — let it land]
PHILIP: "[The punchline or reframe — one sentence]"
[TEXT ON SCREEN: @philipz.fit | [handle]]
```

**Use for:** Threads/TikTok-style punchy content repurposed to Reels. One-liner observations. Myth corrections that don't need elaboration.

**Example beat:**
```
PHILIP: "BMI said I was healthy."
[PAUSE: 1.5 beats]
PHILIP: "My shirt disagreed."
[PAUSE: 1 beat]
PHILIP: "BMI was wrong."
[TEXT ON SCREEN: @philipz.fit]
```

---

### 30-Second — Single Insight

**Structure:**
- Hook (3s) → Setup/context (7s) → The insight or fix (15s) → CTA (5s)

**Beat template:**
```
[ON-CAMERA or VOICE-OVER with visual]
PHILIP: "[Hook — the problem or wrong belief named directly]"
[PAUSE: 1 beat]
PHILIP: "[Setup — why this is happening, in 1–2 sentences]"
[CUT TO or VISUAL CHANGE]
PHILIP: "[The fix or reframe — the single insight, delivered directly]"
[PAUSE: 1 beat]
PHILIP: "[CTA — one action, one sentence]"
[TEXT ON SCREEN: @philipz.fit | Link in bio]
```

**Use for:** Data points, quick myth-busts, habit micro-tips, nutrition one-insight posts.

---

### 60-Second — Mini-Lesson

**Structure:**
- Hook (3s) → Self-implication (7s) → The problem diagnosis (15s) → The fix (25s) → Bridge + CTA (10s)

**Beat template:**
```
[ON-CAMERA]
PHILIP: "[Hook — the wrong belief or relatable failure named directly]"
[PAUSE]
PHILIP: "[Self-implication — Philip's version of the same mistake]"
[CUT TO or B-ROLL suggestion]
PHILIP: "[Diagnosis — why this happens in analytical terms, 2–3 sentences]"
[CUT BACK TO ON-CAMERA or TEXT SLIDE]
PHILIP: "[The fix — specific and direct, step by step if needed]"
[PAUSE]
PHILIP: "[Bridge — sets up the CTA or next piece of content]"
PHILIP: "[CTA — one action]"
[TEXT ON SCREEN: CTA text + @philipz.fit]
```

**Use for:** Full pillar content (any of the 5). The carousel equivalent in video. Best format for repurposing carousel content into a Reel.

---

### 90-Second — Full Story Arc

**Structure:**
- Hook (5s) → The failure story (20s) → The diagnosis (20s) → The turning point (15s) → The fix (20s) → CTA (10s)

**Beat template:**
```
[ON-CAMERA — direct to lens]
PHILIP: "[Hook — the most specific and unexpected version of the failure]"
[PAUSE]
PHILIP: "[The failure story — Philip's personal version, specific details. Failure context before the win.]"
[PAUSE — let the credibility land]
[B-ROLL or CUT if needed]
PHILIP: "[The diagnosis — what was actually wrong, stated plainly]"
[CUT BACK]
PHILIP: "[The turning point — the moment the reframe happened]"
[PAUSE]
PHILIP: "[The fix — what actually worked, step by step]"
[PAUSE]
PHILIP: "[CTA — single action, delivered with conviction]"
[TEXT ON SCREEN: CTA + @philipz.fit + link in bio]
```

**Use for:** High-trust, high-credibility posts. Personal transformation stories. Counterintuitive lessons that require the full arc to land. LinkedIn-style posts translated to video.

## Hook Patterns by Content Pillar

### Pillar 1: Data-Driven Insights
- State the number first, then the implication: *"You lose muscle faster than you gain it. Most people don't know by exactly how much."*
- Correct the wrong premise before stating the right one: *"BMI isn't a health metric. It's a population statistics tool. Those are different things."*
- The "I didn't know this either": *"I tracked calories for a year before I realized I was measuring the wrong thing."*

### Pillar 2: Workout Tips & Micro-Workouts
- Lead with the constraint: *"15 minutes. No equipment. I've done this in a hotel bathroom."*
- The contrast hook: *"The gym bros spend 90 minutes. I spend 40. I'm making better progress."*
- The permission hook: *"You don't need to go to the gym. Here's what you actually need."*

### Pillar 3: Nutrition & Protein Strategy
- The number that triggers panic: *"You need about 160 grams of protein a day. Most guys eat about 70."*
- The myth-bust opener: *"Eating every 2 hours to boost your metabolism is not how metabolism works."*
- The permission reframe: *"You don't need a meal plan. You need a protein target and one rule."*

### Pillar 4: Consistency & Habit Systems
- The accurate reframe: *"You're not lazy. Your approach is broken. Different problem, different fix."*
- The self-implication: *"I skipped 3 weeks in a row last year. Here's what I actually did to get back."*
- The pattern insight: *"The reason you quit every January is the same reason your gym bag has been sitting by the door since October."*

### Pillar 5: Body Image, Confidence & Gay Culture
- The self-implication opener: *"I avoided pools for years because I didn't want anyone to see me without a shirt. The fix wasn't abs."*
- The honest post-transformation hook: *"Getting leaner didn't fix my confidence. Here's what actually did."*
- The ambivalence hook: *"We all feel the pressure. Almost nobody says it out loud."*

This pillar leads with Philip's own avoidance or insecurity, non-negotiably — see `cpz-research/references/content-pillars.md` Pillar 5 for the full core stance and platform explicitness guide before scripting.

## Pacing & Delivery Notes

**Dry delivery is the brand:** Philip doesn't hype. He observes. The energy is confident and slightly deadpan — like someone who's already made the mistake explaining why it doesn't work, not a coach yelling at a client to push harder.

**Deliberate pauses are not dead air.** A 1–2 second pause after a punchline or an uncomfortable truth is intentional. It signals: "that one lands, sit with it."

**No filler sounds:** No "um," no "so yeah," no trailing off. Philip's written voice is precise — the spoken voice should match. Edit out any hedging.

**Speed:** Normal conversational pace. Not slow and dramatic, not rapid-fire. The analytical ICA follows precise speech at normal pace. He doesn't need it simplified.

## Caption Guidance for Reels

Reel captions are **shorter than carousel captions** because the video carries the content.

| Platform | Caption Length | Format |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Instagram Reels | 50–100 words | Hook line → 1–2 sentences of context → single CTA |
| TikTok | 30–60 words | Punchy. Hook. One sentence. CTA. |

**Rule:** The caption amplifies the video — it does not repeat it. Use the caption for the one thing the video didn't have room to say.
